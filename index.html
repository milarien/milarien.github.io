Overview

Large language models are increasingly deployed in medicine, finance, law, and government. In these domains, the critical failure is not fluency or intelligence â€” it is epistemic illegitimacy: the inability to determine whether a conclusion is permitted by the available evidence structure.

Current LLM systems default to collapsing ambiguity into a single answer, even when multiple interpretations remain structurally admissible. In regulated environments, this creates liability exposure.

Aurora-Lens introduces a governance layer that operates at the transport boundary between applications and LLM providers. It preserves parallel admissible interpretations, enforces constraint-based collapse, and produces a tamper-evident forensic audit trail suitable for regulatory scrutiny.
