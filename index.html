Title: Geometry-Driven Conceptual Processing: A Dual-Mode Language Architecture for Efficient and Scalable AI Reasoning
Date: November 2025

Overview

This whitepaper introduces a geometric, dual-mode language architecture that separates structural parsing from semantic representation and replaces glyph-based tokenization with non-lexical conceptual units. Concepts are organized within a layered semantic topology, allowing models to reason over meaning directly rather than over surface-form sequences.

The result is a system that has the potential to deliver:

  • Significant reductions in compute cost and inference latency
  • Improved containment of hallucination drift
  • Scalability that tracks conceptual density rather than token length
  • Smaller models with large-model reasoning capabilities
  • More robust multilingual and domain-general behavior

Intended Audience

This work is intended for researchers, architects, and decision-makers working on large language models, inference optimization, and next-generation AI systems, particularly where GPU cost, scalability, and semantic stability are critical constraints.
